Indiana ranks 5th in the nation for soybeans production, with approximately 5.3 million acres planted annually. The state's soybeans is primarily used for livestock feed, ethanol production, and export.

The top soybeans producing counties ‚Äî Tippecanoe, White, Benton, Warren, Jasper ‚Äî benefit from rich soils and favorable growing conditions. Indiana farmers typically plant soybeans from late April through May, with harvest occurring in October and November.
