Nebraska ranks 3rd in the nation for wheat production, with approximately 9.4 million acres planted annually. The state's wheat is primarily used for livestock feed, ethanol production, and export.

The top wheat producing counties ‚Äî Dawson, Lincoln, Phelps, Custer, Buffalo ‚Äî benefit from rich soils and favorable growing conditions. Nebraska farmers typically plant wheat from late April through May, with harvest occurring in October and November.

Nebraska wheat basis tends to be weaker than Iowa or Illinois due to greater distance from major export terminals and ethanol plants. However, local demand from dairy operations provides some basis support in certain regions.

Marketing Considerations for NE Farmers

  • Basis typically improves 15-20¬¢ from harvest to summer
  • On-farm storage can capture this basis improvement
  • Watch for local ethanol plant demand spikes
  • River terminal bids may offer better basis for southern NE
