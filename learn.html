Understanding commodity markets and agricultural economics.

üìà How Commodity Prices Work

Commodity prices are determined by supply and demand on exchanges like the Chicago Mercantile Exchange (CME). Futures contracts allow farmers to lock in prices for future delivery, managing price risk.

Key factors affecting prices: weather, global demand, transportation costs, government policies, and currency exchange rates.

üåΩ Basis Explained

Basis = Local Cash Price - Futures Price

Basis reflects local supply/demand conditions and transportation costs. A strong (less negative) basis benefits sellers; a weak (more negative) basis benefits buyers. Local basis typically ranges from -$0.15 to -$0.50 for corn depending on location and time of year.

üõ°Ô∏è Crop Insurance Basics

Federal crop insurance protects against yield loss and price drops. Common plans include Revenue Protection (RP), which covers revenue guarantee based on your APH and projected price, and Yield Protection (YP), which covers yield only.

Deadlines: Sales closing dates vary by crop and region. Check with RMA or your crop insurance agent.
