üìä Understanding Cash Bids & Basis

Cash Bid = The price a local elevator will pay you for grain today

Basis = The difference between the local cash price and the futures price

Cash Price = Futures Price + Basis

Example: If March corn futures are $4.50 and your local elevator has a basis of -$0.35, your cash bid is $4.15/bu

üìà Narrow Basis = Better Price

A basis of -$0.20 is better than -$0.40. Watch for basis to narrow (get closer to zero) before selling.

üìÖ Basis Changes Seasonally

Basis typically widens at harvest (more supply) and narrows in spring/summer. Historical patterns help predict moves.

üöö Location Matters

Elevators closer to end users (ethanol plants, river terminals) often have stronger basis than remote locations.
