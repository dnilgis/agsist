Field Scout — draw a field, get its whole story

Search your address or pan to your ground, trace the field, and read what the land is: every soil type and its productivity, the last five years of crops grown on it, live weather and drought, and the closest cash bids. All from public data. No login, no cost.

Each panel below loads on its own from a separate public source (USDA, Open-Meteo, US Drought Monitor). If one says a government server isn’t responding, that source is just having a moment — the rest still work, and redrawing in a few minutes usually clears it.

Field Scout reads public survey data for the area you draw — it doesn’t know who owns the land. Saved fields are stored on your device only, never on our servers. Soil and crop layers are survey estimates, not a substitute for sampling your own ground. Nothing here is financial advice.
Sources: USDA SSURGO soil survey · USDA Cropland Data Layer · Open-Meteo · US Drought Monitor · AGSIST cash-bid feed
