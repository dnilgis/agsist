Frequently Asked Questions
Which grain elevators does AGSIST cover?
AGSIST displays cash bids from elevators, co-ops, and grain facilities across the Corn Belt, Great Plains, and Gulf Coast sourced from the Barchart OnDemand API. Coverage includes independent elevators, co-operative grain facilities, and major grain company locations. Enter your ZIP code on the cash bids page to see available bids near you.
How do I find my local elevator’s grain price on AGSIST?
Go to agsist.com/cash-bids and enter your 5-digit ZIP code. AGSIST will show available corn, soybean, and wheat bids from grain facilities within your area, sorted by distance. Bids are sourced from Barchart OnDemand and updated throughout the trading day.
How current are the cash bid prices?
Cash bids are sourced from the Barchart OnDemand API and reflect elevator-posted prices updated during trading hours. Prices may lag real-time futures by minutes depending on when the elevator last posted. Always confirm the current bid directly with the elevator before loading or hauling grain.
My elevator isn’t showing up. What can I do?
Coverage depends on whether your elevator posts bids through the Barchart data network. Some smaller independent elevators may not participate. If your elevator isn’t listed, email sig@farmers1st.com — I’ll investigate whether it’s available and can be added. I’m actively working to expand coverage across the Corn Belt.
Is AGSIST affiliated with any elevator or grain company?
No. AGSIST is an independent agricultural intelligence dashboard built and operated by Sigurd Lindquist, a crop insurance agent and grain farmer. It has no ownership, financial, or commercial relationship with any elevator, co-op, or grain company. Cash bid data is sourced neutrally from the Barchart OnDemand API.
