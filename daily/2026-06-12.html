🌾GRAINS END WEEK LOWERMEDIUM CONVICTION
📡DRIVERUSDA winter wheat cut to smallest crop since 1965, managed money liquidation ahead of WASDE
↺Grains: defensive thesis played out in managed money data, liquidation confirmed.
Corn led the slide, down 1.75 cents to $4.10 and **15,000 contracts of managed money liquidation** since Monday. Soybeans held flat at $11.13 but November gave back three-quarters of a cent. Wheat fell hardest, off 6.25 cents to $5.83 despite USDA cutting winter wheat production another 2% Thursday. The carry's working in corn: December at $4.37 holds a 27-cent premium to July, widest it's been since April. Fund positioning ahead of Tuesday's WASDE is keeping the pressure on.
Five-day slide complete, funds waiting for Tuesday's WASDE to reset positioning.
🐂CATTLE COMPLEX REVERSES HIGHERMEDIUM CONVICTION
📡DRIVERSeventh screwworm case confirmed Tuesday, uncertainty premium removed from cattle complex
↺Cattle: narrative held exactly as called, momentum building on screwworm clarity.
Feeders ran 1.4% to $359.52, the strongest day in two weeks after **Tuesday's seventh screwworm case confirmation** removed the uncertainty that had been weighing on the complex. Live cattle added modest gains to $242.62, up four-tenths of a percent. The screwworm story is now priced: seven confirmed cases in South Texas, USDA response underway with sterile fly releases. Cattle basis is firming in the Plains as the uncertainty discount disappears. The feeder/live ratio at 1.48 is back to pre-outbreak levels.
Disease clarity ends cattle discount, complex finding footing above recent lows.
🛢️ENERGY FALLS ON DIPLOMACYMEDIUM CONVICTION
📡DRIVERIran-U.S. diplomatic progress via Swiss intermediaries deflating Hormuz premium
WTI crude tumbled 2% to $84.58, the biggest single-day drop in three weeks as **Iran-U.S. diplomatic progress via Swiss intermediaries** deflated the Strait of Hormuz premium. Natural gas slipped 1.2% to $3.04 on storage builds ahead of peak demand season. The energy complex is unwinding the geopolitical premium that built through May, with crude losing roughly 19% from its $105 peak as tensions ease. Front-month crude trading at 46% of its 52-week range tells you the war premium is mostly gone.
Geopolitical premium unwinding faster than supply fundamentals can support prices.
📈EQUITIES LEAD MACRO HIGHERLOW CONVICTION
📡DRIVERS&P 500 overnight surprise +1.8%, strongest session in two weeks
S&P 500 ran 1.8% to $7,394, the biggest day in two weeks and **an overnight surprise that carried through the session**. Dollar index held flat at $99.77, still near 52-week highs but not adding pressure. Gold ticked higher to $4,227 as rate cut expectations firmed on softer economic data. The equity bounce is rotating money back into risk assets, which typically supports commodity demand, but grain funds aren't participating yet.
Risk-on rotation supporting commodities broadly, but grain funds still waiting.

🧠 THE MORE YOU KNOW
Why the corn carry tells you more than the close
December corn trading 27 cents over July isn't just a calendar spread-it's the market's vote on harvest abundance. When the carry gets this wide, it's pricing storage as profitable and telling you funds expect lower prices through October. The 27-cent gap is the widest since April and matches the carry structure from 2023's large crop year. Front-month weakness with wide carry means the market is pricing time as an enemy, not a friend.

The challenge comes as 2026-2027 renewable volume obligations hit final form, potentially reshaping biomass diesel production requirements that underpin soybean oil demand.
POLICY
Marshall Withdraws Prop 12 Sponsorship
Sen. Roger Marshall dropped co-sponsorship of the Save Our Bacon Act that would negate California's Proposition 12. The Kansas Republican's move signals shifting politics around livestock housing mandates as the pork industry adapts to compliance requirements rather than fighting them in Congress.
TRADE
Trump Questions USMCA Renewal
President said Wednesday the U.S. might not renew the free trade agreement with Canada and Mexico, criticizing trade deficits with both countries. Agricultural groups at House hearings called for USMCA continuation with minor modifications, but uncertainty around the 2026 review is building.
