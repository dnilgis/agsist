HOGS CRASH NINE PERCENT AS WEEK ENDS MIXED

Cattle breakout stalls while energy reverses Friday's crude selloff.

Lean hogs crashed 9.2% to $92.83 Friday, the worst single-day drop in eight weeks and a brutal end to what started as cattle's breakout week. The livestock complex is splitting: cattle still holding above $250 but momentum's gone, hogs getting hammered on seasonal pressure, and dairy marking time at $17.08. Friday's close says the commodity rally that started Monday is running out of conviction.

🐄Livestock Complex SplitsMEDIUM CONVICTION
Cattle closed Friday at $253.00, down just 0.4% but the breakout momentum that started Monday is clearly fading. The funds got their test above $250 but aren't adding conviction — open interest is flat this week while price marked time. Feeders dropped 0.6% to $371.40, tracking the front month lower. Hogs are the disaster story: $92.83 represents a complete reversal from Monday's optimism and puts the contract back near April lows. Spring farrowing data is starting to bite, and the seasonal pressure into summer is real.
Cattle holding but not advancing, hogs collapsing on seasonal reality.
🎯 Cattle above $250: momentum's gone but support holds. Hogs below $95: seasonal pressure accelerating.
🌽Grains Mark Time AgainLOW CONVICTION
Corn closed the week at $4.68, down 2.0% Friday and giving back most of Wednesday's weather premium. December corn at $4.99 held better, up 0.3% as new crop carries a 31-cent premium to old crop — carry's working but the board's not pricing any spring planting stress. Soybeans fell 1.2% to $11.88 while November beans gained 0.3% to $11.83, an unusual inverse that says demand for old crop is thin. Wheat dropped 2.8% to $6.25 on export disappointment. The grain complex is back to marking time while planting season peaks.
Carry spreads working but weather premium evaporating fast.
⚡Energy WhipsawsLOW CONVICTION
Crude reversed Thursday's 4.4% crash with a 3.3% decline to $101.94 Friday, proving the energy complex can't find direction. The $95-$105 range that's held since March is still intact but the whipsaw action says nobody's convinced about direction. Natural gas barely moved at $2.78, still trapped near the 52-week low as storage builds continue. The energy complex that exploded Monday and crashed Thursday finished the week exactly where it started — confused and directionless.
Energy range-bound and confused after the week's whipsaw moves.
🧠 THE MORE YOU KNOW
Why Single-Day Crashes Reset Sentiment
Friday's 9.2% hog crash demonstrates how one brutal session can undo weeks of building momentum. When a commodity drops more than 5% in a single day, it typically takes three to five sessions for buyers to re-emerge, regardless of fundamentals. The psychological impact of watching $8,000 disappear from a standard contract changes trader behavior immediately. Studies show that single-day crashes above 7% reset fund positioning for at least a full week, which explains why livestock momentum stalled so completely after Monday's optimism.
