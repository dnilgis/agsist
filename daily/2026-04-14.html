🧠 THE MORE YOU KNOW
Why Every Planting Day Matters Now
University research consistently shows that corn planted after April 20th in southern Minnesota and Wisconsin loses roughly 1 bushel per acre for every day of delay. This isn't just academic theory — it's based on decades of yield data accounting for growing degree days and frost risk. The relationship becomes even steeper after May 1st, when yield potential drops 1.5-2 bushels per day in many areas. That's why grain markets become hypersensitive to weather forecasts during the next month.
