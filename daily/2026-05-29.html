CATTLE COMPLEX SPLITS WIDER AS WEEK ENDS

Live cattle breaks $242 floor while feeders hold $352, resolving week's convergence question through weakness.

🧵 FRIDAY RESOLUTIONWill the cattle complex split resolve through feeder strength or live cattle weakness?

The cattle complex answered this week's question the hard way: live cattle broke $242 support to close at $241.15, down half a percent, while feeders held $352.88 on the same decline. The 4.6% spread between live and feeders that looked ready to compress Monday is now widening again. Five days of watching for convergence through strength got convergence through synchronized weakness instead.

🐂Cattle Break Support TogetherMEDIUM CONVICTION
📡DRIVERBoxed beef values sharply lower, pulling cash cattle with futures
↺Cattle: yesterday's Iran ease thesis played out, oil's 19% May drop confirmed.
Live cattle closed $241.15, breaking the $242 floor that held for three sessions and settling at the lowest close in two weeks. Feeders dropped the same half-percent to $352.88 but stayed above their $350 technical level. **The convergence trade died through weakness, not strength.** Boxed beef was sharply lower during the session, pulling cash values with it. The weekly cattle on feed report due Tuesday will reset the fundamental story, but the chart damage is already done.
Support broken, convergence through weakness, Tuesday's report resets the story.
🌽Grains Drift in Memorial LullLOW CONVICTION
Corn closed $4.5275, down 3 cents in the thinnest trade of the week. Soybeans eased a penny to $11.98. Chicago wheat managed a quarter-cent gain to $6.23, the only green on the board. **No catalyst worth naming, just Memorial Day weekend position-squaring.** The eastern Belt weather forecast shows cooler, drier conditions next week, but planting pace is already ahead of normal and the prevented plant deadline is still a week away. Range-bound consolidation, not direction.
Holiday drift, no weather premium yet, prevented plant deadline still a week out.

📍 BASIS PULSE
Eastern Belt corn basis steady despite futures weakness
**Local corn basis holding firm** as Memorial Day weekend thins trade but doesn't kill demand. Ethanol plants stayed online through the holiday and river logistics keep moving old-crop. Western Belt staying softer, consistent with the seasonal. The basis bid is telling you the cash market isn't following futures lower yet.
🧠 THE MORE YOU KNOW
The Memorial Day fade: why holiday Fridays drain conviction
Corn down 3 cents on the year's thinnest trade tells the Memorial Day story: nobody wants to hold risk into a three-day weekend. Holiday Fridays consistently show position-squaring rather than new conviction, even when fundamentals argue for moves. The pros know Monday's first hour will reset whatever Friday's thin trade decided. Today's price action was about calendar, not catalysts.
