FEEDERS MOVE HIGHER 3.2%, CATTLE COMPLEX SNAPS HIGHER

Cargill lockout pressure finally breaks, live cattle follow feeders up 1.6% in the biggest session since May.

🧵 FRIDAY RESOLUTIONWill the grain liquidation finish before weather takes control of the story?
⚡Overnight Surprises: Feeder Cattle UP 3.2% / Live Cattle UP 1.6%

Feeder cattle closed $353.38, up 3.2% in the biggest session since the Cargill lockout began. Live cattle followed with a 1.6% gain to $241.68, both contracts bouncing hard off Wednesday's lows on reports the Fort Morgan situation might finally resolve. The grain liquidation that dominated the week stayed liquid-corn lost another penny to $4.21-but cattle just answered the week's question: liquidation finished, weather takes control next week.

🐂CATTLE COMPLEX RUNS HIGHERHIGH CONVICTION
Feeders **ran 3.2%** to $353.38, the biggest session since the Cargill lockout began eleven days ago. Live cattle followed with a 1.6% gain to $241.68, both contracts reversing Wednesday's breakdown in convincing fashion. The **lockout premium is deflating**-reports suggest Fort Morgan negotiations are advancing, though no official resolution yet. Funds that sold the processing bottleneck are covering into strength. The feeder/live ratio tightened to 1.46 from 1.48 Wednesday, normal seasonal behavior when processing constraints ease.
Processing bottleneck premium unwinding, both contracts bouncing hard.
🌾GRAINS FINISH LIQUIDATION WEEKMEDIUM CONVICTION
📡DRIVERNo specific catalyst, fund liquidation completing weekly selling cycle
↺Grains: liquidation extended exactly as flagged, wheat showing first resistance
Corn closed $4.21, down another penny in the **fourth straight session** of fund selling. Soybeans held $11.28, off just a penny, but the Dec contract lost 1.5 cents as the carry widened again. Wheat bucked the trend, gaining 2 cents to $5.83 on short covering ahead of the weekend. **Open interest dropped 15,000 contracts** across the grain complex Friday-somebody finished taking profits. The planting season behind-schedule premium is gone, replaced by normal June weather watch.
Fund selling cycle completes, normal June weather watch begins Monday.
⛽CRUDE LOSES IRAN PREMIUMMEDIUM CONVICTION
📡DRIVERContinued Iran-Hormuz diplomatic progress via Swiss intermediaries
WTI crude closed $92.86, down 30 cents as **Iran-Hormuz tensions continue easing** on diplomatic progress. The strait premium that built since early April is deflating methodically-crude lost 19% in May and gave back another 0.3% today. Natural gas dropped 1.1% to $3.31 on storage builds and mild weather forecasts. Energy's the mirror image of cattle: geopolitical premium deflating while processing premiums (refineries running hard into summer) firm the crack spreads.

🧠 THE MORE YOU KNOW
Why lockout premiums snap back faster than they build
Today's 3.2% feeder move higher on lockout resolution reports shows why processing bottlenecks create asymmetric risk. The Cargill Fort Morgan lockout took eleven days to drive feeders down 8%, but just one session of resolution hope brought back 3.2%. Markets price worst-case scenarios into processing disruptions, then unwind the premium violently when normal operations return. The 1,700 locked-out workers represent 2% of weekly US slaughter capacity-small in aggregate, massive in local markets where the plants operate.
