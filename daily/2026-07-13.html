🐖Hogs Had Their Biggest DayMEDIUM CONVICTION
📡DRIVERU.S.-Iran weekend escalation, Strait of Hormuz closure claims, risk-on fund rotation into protein exports.
Lean hogs ran to $94.78, up 10.8%, their biggest single-session move in months, and nothing in Friday's close telegraphed it. This is an overnight surprise with a specific driver: Iran's weekend strikes on Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman, and Jordan, the U.S.-Iran re-escalation, and the Strait of Hormuz closing again lifted every risk-on commodity that has a short-term demand or export story. Pork exports move through different lanes than LNG, but funds rotating into commodities with export leverage found hogs. The CME Group also announced two new beef trim futures contracts Friday, a structural signal that the cattle supply chain is adding risk-management tools, and that's creating positive sentiment across the protein complex. $94.78 is the highest hog print in weeks. The question is whether this holds Tuesday or gives back half the move when the Hormuz headlines stop being new news.
Real move, real catalyst, but 11% in a day demands skepticism about follow-through.

🧠 THE MORE YOU KNOW
When the Strait Closes: How Hormuz Moves Your Input Costs
The Strait of Hormuz carries roughly 20% of global LNG and a significant share of crude, and tanker traffic just fell to a five-week low as transponders went dark again. That's not a grain story directly, but it becomes one inside 30 to 60 days. Diesel, nitrogen fertilizer, and crop-protection chemicals all trace back to natural gas and crude feedstocks. When Hormuz premium rebuilds, the sequence runs: crude higher, gas higher, ammonia and urea higher, then fall input bid sheets tighten. Producers locking fall anhydrous or UAN contracts this week are pricing before that sequence plays out. Today's WTI close at $71.41 is down on the day but opened 4% higher this morning: the market hasn't decided yet whether this escalation sticks.
