Funds in liquidation mode as growing season begins with little drama.

🐷Hogs Break Key $100 FloorMEDIUM CONVICTION
📡DRIVERNo clean catalyst, looks like fund liquidation ahead of summer
Lean hogs dropped 2.5% to $99.50, the first close under $100 since March and the clearest sign that **summer liquidation is real**. No news drove it, just managed money stepping out ahead of June's lighter data flow. The break through $100 psychological support puts the next test at $97, where seasonal demand typically shows up. Pork cutout values haven't cracked, this is purely positioning.
First sub-$100 close in three months signals fund exodus, not demand weakness.
🌾Grains Mark Time Into Growing SeasonLOW CONVICTION
📡DRIVERUSDA Crop Progress due Monday 3:00 PM, market positioning for update
Corn eased to $4.46, beans added a quarter-cent to $11.92, and wheat ticked up a penny to $6.14. **Nobody's making moves** with crop conditions still developing and no weather premium in sight. December corn at $4.75 shows the market's pricing normal yields, normal weather, normal everything. The real action starts when pollination weather shows up in July, not now.
Range-bound consolidation while waiting for weather to matter.
🥣Oats Hit Hardest in Grain ComplexLOW CONVICTION
Oats fell 2.6% to $3.47, the day's biggest grain loser as **thin trade amplified selling pressure**. Open interest in oats is a fraction of corn or wheat, so when the few funds positioned there decide to book profits, the moves get outsized. At 57% of the 52-week range, oats are pricing in adequate supplies without the weather premium that might develop later in summer.
Thin trade makes small selling look big, no fundamental shift.
⛽Crude Holds Above $90 Despite Iran RevisionsMEDIUM CONVICTION
WTI gained $0.80 to $90.51 after Trump sent the Iran peace agreement back for revisions, but the **geopolitical premium stayed capped** rather than rebuilding. The Iran-Israel conflict that's roiled energy markets since April cooled with diplomatic progress, but the market's learned not to price full disruption risk until it sees actual supply cuts, not just diplomatic friction. Russia's jet fuel export ban through November adds some supply tightness, but that's aviation fuel, not crude.
Iran tensions contained, crude holding $90 without rebuilding premium.
⇄ THE SPREAD TO WATCH
December corn / July corn carry
29 cents carry, normal seasonal
The 29-cent carry from July to December corn is **exactly where the seasonal says it should be**, pricing normal yields and normal harvest pressure. When this spread starts moving, it'll tell you whether the market's pricing weather risk or harvest abundance before the weather does.
📍 BASIS PULSE
Eastern Belt corn basis steady, western locations soft
Corn basis east of the Mississippi **holding firm** as ethanol plants return from maintenance turnarounds. Western Belt locations staying soft with ample farmer selling and rail capacity opening up. The spread between eastern and western basis is back to seasonal normal after May's tightness.
🧠 THE MORE YOU KNOW
Why $100 hogs matter more than the round number
Today's break below $100 lean hogs isn't about psychology, it's about **positioning mechanics**. Managed money holds its biggest hog long since 2023, nearly 40,000 contracts net long. When funds liquidate concentrated positions in thin contracts like hogs, the moves amplify. The $100 level matters because it's where technical stops cluster, and breaking it forces the next wave of selling. That's why today's 2.5% drop happened with no news catalyst-it's the positioning, not the pork.
