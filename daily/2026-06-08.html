GRAINS EXTEND SLIDE, SILVER FALLS SHARPLY FIVE PERCENT

Corn hits four-week low as dollar strength overwhelms commodity complex.

🧵 MONDAY SETUPWill the dollar break 100 and drag commodities into a deeper correction?
⚡Overnight Surprises: Silver DN 5.2% / S&P 500 DN 2.6% / Gold DN 2.3% / Lean Hogs DN 2.8% / Soybean Oil DN 3.1% / US Dollar Index UP 0.7% / Oats DN 2.9%

Corn dropped to $4.17, the lowest close since mid-May, as selling that started last Tuesday shows no signs of stopping. The dollar's 0.7% rise to $100.07 is pulling money out of everything that isn't denominated in greenbacks, and grain futures are getting hit hardest. Silver's 5.2% fall to $69.10 tells the story: when the dollar flexes, commodities fold.

🌾GRAINS EXTEND FOUR-DAY SLIDEMEDIUM CONVICTION
📡DRIVERDollar Index rose 0.7% to $100.07, pulling funds out of commodities
Corn fell another 4½ cents to $4.17, the **fourth straight session lower** and the lowest close since mid-May. Soybeans dropped to $11.21, now sitting 77 cents off the April highs. The selling has no clean catalyst beyond dollar strength and fund liquidation. **Crop conditions remain favorable** across the Belt, rainfall adequate, and planting wrapped up ahead of schedule. This is position unwinding, not fundamental deterioration.
Selling is technical, not fundamental; crops are fine.
🥈METALS FALL ON DOLLAR MOVE HIGHERHIGH CONVICTION
📡DRIVERDollar Index gained 0.7% toward 100 level, triggering algorithmic commodity selling
Silver broke down 5.2% to $69.10, the **biggest single-day drop since February**, as dollar strength overwhelmed industrial demand. Gold fell 2.3% to $4,365, confirming this is currency-driven selling, not metal-specific weakness. The Dollar Index at $100.07 is approaching the psychological century mark that triggers algorithmic selling in commodities. **When the dollar runs, metals run for the exits first.**
Dollar at 100 is the line; metals break first when it gets there.
🐷HOGS FALL ON SCREWWORM FEARSMEDIUM CONVICTION
📡DRIVERSecond New World screwworm case confirmed in Texas, prompting Canadian import ban
↺Hogs: screwworm news adds fundamental pressure to technical selling.
Lean hogs dropped 2.8% to $98.80 as **New World screwworm cases in Texas** prompted Canada to ban livestock imports from the state. Two calves and a dog tested positive for the flesh-eating pest, the second confirmed case in South Texas within six miles of the first. USDA is ramping up border trapping and several states are implementing temporary import restrictions. The psychological impact is outweighing the actual supply disruption so far.
Disease fears bigger than actual supply impact, so far.
⇄ THE SPREAD TO WATCH
July corn / December corn carry
29 cents wide, holding steady
The carry isn't breaking down despite four days of selling, which means this is fund liquidation, not storage pressure. If fundamentals were deteriorating, the front month would be leading lower and the carry would blow wider. Instead, both months are falling together.
📍 BASIS PULSE
Eastern Corn Belt basis steady despite futures slide
**Illinois River basis holding flat** while futures drop tells you local demand is solid. Ethanol plants are still bidding for nearby bushels even as the futures board gets hit. Western Corn Belt staying soft, consistent with the seasonal and longer haul to processors.
🧠 THE MORE YOU KNOW
Why the dollar at 100 matters for grain prices
The Dollar Index hit $100.07 today, approaching the century mark that triggers algorithmic selling programs in commodity futures. **When DXY crosses 100, systematic funds automatically reduce commodity exposure** because their risk models flag currency headwinds. It's not fundamental analysis, it's programmed math. The 2022 grain rally stalled when the dollar broke 110. The 2015 crop price breakdown accelerated when DXY crossed 100 and stayed there. Today's 17-cent corn decline in four days fits the pattern: systematic selling, not weather or export news.
