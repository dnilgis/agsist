HOGS LEAD CHARGE AS GRAINS STABILIZE

Lean hogs added 1.8% while corn and beans found footing after Memorial liquidation.

🧵 WED UPDATEWill the grain liquidation finish before weather takes control of the story?

Lean hogs ran to $101.50, up 1.8% in the session's biggest livestock move, as Class III milk followed with a 3.1% overnight move that held through the close at $17.25. The grain complex found its feet after five straight days of fund liquidation, corn adding 3 cents to $4.41 and beans firming 3½ cents to $11.72. Yesterday's methodical selling pressure took a breather, but volume stayed thin and the funds haven't committed new length.

🐄LIVESTOCK LEADS HIGHERMEDIUM CONVICTION
📡DRIVERClass III milk technical breakout overnight, butter production margins staying premium
↺Livestock: broke sideways drift exactly as thin volume suggested it would
**Lean hogs broke the livestock stalemate**, adding $1.84 to $101.50 on technical buying after three days of sideways drift. Class III milk led overnight with a 3.1% move that held through the session, closing $17.25 as **butter margins stay premium** heading into peak cooling season. Live cattle slipped 40 cents to $239.80 but held above Monday's $238 test, while feeders gave back $2.10 to $348.90. The **livestock complex is pricing summer demand** while grains mark time.
Hogs and milk lead livestock higher as summer demand patterns emerge.
🌽GRAINS PAUSE LIQUIDATIONLOW CONVICTION
📡DRIVERUSDA's Vaden expects China to honor soybean purchase commitments this year
Corn firmed 1½ cents to $4.41 and beans added 3½ cents to $11.72, the first meaningful bounce since Memorial Day fund liquidation began. **Volume stayed thin**, managed money still sidelined after five straight sessions of selling. Chicago wheat gained 6½ cents to $6.06 on technical buying, but the move lacks conviction with **no new weather premium** building despite scattered Plains showers. Deputy Ag Secretary Vaden's comments on China soybean orders provided a headline but no fresh tonnage.
First bounce since Memorial liquidation, but funds haven't committed new money.
🛢️CRUDE HOLDS IRAN GAINSMEDIUM CONVICTION
📡DRIVERIranian drone strikes on Kuwait International Airport overnight, escalating regional tensions
WTI crude added $1.54 to $96.20, holding most of Monday's 5% Iran escalation premium as **overnight strikes on Kuwait airport** kept geopolitical risk bid alive. Natural gas eased a penny to $3.16 as domestic supply concerns fade with seasonal demand still weeks away. The **crude complex is pricing convoy risk** through the Persian Gulf shipping lanes, not fundamental supply disruption. Iran tensions, ongoing since early April, shifted from Strait of Hormuz threats to direct strikes on Gulf state infrastructure.
Crude holds Iran premium as Gulf tensions escalate beyond shipping lanes.
⇄ THE SPREAD TO WATCH
July corn / December corn carry
27 cents inverse, narrowing from 31 cents Monday
The inversion is shrinking as new crop December gains on old crop July, signaling the market expects normal weather through pollination. When the carry flips positive, weather premium season is over and harvest pressure pricing begins.
📍 BASIS PULSE
Eastern Corn Belt firming on ethanol restart demand
**Illinois and Indiana elevators** are adding 2-4 cents to posted bids as ethanol plants restart after maintenance shutdowns. Western Belt staying soft with farmer selling light ahead of June tax deadlines. The **regional split** reflects logistics more than fundamental corn demand, but eastern producers with old-crop storage have a window the futures board isn't pricing.
🧠 THE MORE YOU KNOW
Why thin volume tells the liquidation story better than price
Today's 3-cent corn bounce came on volume 40% below the 20-day average, typical of a pause not a reversal. Real fund re-entry shows up as volume gains before price moves, as position sizes matter more than direction to managed money. When the selling resumes, look for volume to confirm it. When buying starts, volume will lead price by at least a session.
