CRUDE RUNS 6%, CORN SPLIT WIDENS, BEANS HOLD

Trump's Iran blockade reinstatement hit oil overnight; corn carries the pollination discount forward while soybeans push back toward $12.10 November resistance.

⚡Overnight Surprise: WTI Crude Oil UP 5.9%

Crude oil had its biggest single-session gain in months, running nearly 6% to $78.66 after President Trump reinstated the Iran blockade Monday, resetting the energy premium that had been quietly deflating for weeks. The corn market isn't listening: nearby held at $4.41 while December fell another 4.5 cents to $4.63, the old-crop/new-crop split now 22 cents wide and widening. The weekly thread question is alive: does Monday's crop progress confirm the good-crop thesis, or does field-level stress put the weather premium back on the board? Right now, beans are the market with an answer.

The 22-cent split is still the only honest weather premium calculator in this market.
🫘Soybeans Push, Meal FadesMEDIUM CONVICTION
📡DRIVERSoybean oil running 2.1% on biofuel demand; meal weakness signals feed-side softness; Missouri field reports show strong bean conditions vs. stressed corn.
↺Beans: oil/meal divergence now the dominant intra-complex story.
Soybeans nearby ran to $12.07, up more than 10 cents, while November held at $11.94 and pulled back a fraction. The divergence inside the complex is the real tell: soybean oil ran 2.1% to $71.09 while meal dropped 1.2% to $315.60. That is not a demand story, it is a crush composition story. The biofuel and vegetable oil side of crush is bidding for beans; the feed side is not. Missouri farmers say soybeans look as good as ever despite the dry stretch hitting corn, which is a bearish data point for November if field reports are correct. The $12.10 November level is the line for new-crop bulls; today's close at $11.94 says the market didn't get there but wants another shot. Thursday's export sales are the catalyst that either provides the excuse to push through or confirms the consolidation.
Oil leading, meal lagging: this is a biofuel bid, not a broad demand story.
🎯 Unpriced new-crop soybeans: hold into Thursday export sales. Above 400K MT exports, $12.10 November is the scale-sale window. Below 300K MT, the bid fades and storage economics dominate.

🧠 THE MORE YOU KNOW
The soybean crush: when oil and meal stop moving together, the market is telling you something.
Today soybean oil ran 2.1% to $71.09 while soybean meal dropped 1.2% to $315.60. Normally they move roughly together because both come out of the same bean. When they split hard, it means one end of the crush equation has a demand driver the other doesn't. Right now, renewable diesel mandates and biodiesel blending demand are bidding aggressively for soy oil, while global protein feed demand has cooled. The crush miller captures that spread: buy the bean, sell both products, pocket the margin. What it means for producers is that bean demand is real but narrow. If the biofuel policy tailwind under current EPA blending standards softens, so does this bid. The 52-week high positioning of nearby beans at 94% from the low is largely a biofuel story. Keep that in mind when pricing new crop.
