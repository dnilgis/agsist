üìà WHAT MOVES CORN PRICES

üå¶Ô∏è Weather

The biggest price driver during the growing season. Key periods: planting (April-May), pollination (July), and grain fill (August). Drought stress during pollination can devastate yields and spike prices.

üìä USDA Reports

Monthly WASDE reports move markets instantly. Key annual reports: Prospective Plantings (March), Acreage (June), and quarterly Grain Stocks. Check our USDA calendar.

üö¢ Export Demand

China, Mexico, and Japan are top corn importers. Large export sales (reported weekly) support prices. Trade policy and global production (Brazil, Argentina) affect US competitiveness.

‚õΩ Ethanol

About 35-40% of US corn goes to ethanol production. Ethanol margins, RFS mandates, and gasoline demand all affect corn consumption. Weekly ethanol production reports influence prices.
